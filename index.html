Why this isn't another screen-time camp

Two routes, one expedition

A cohort that meets your kid where they are

Students are grouped by age so the depth fits. Younger Explorers build intuition; older Explorers get into the real math and modern systems.

Explorers A · Grades 4–5 · Ages 9–11

Understanding AI

For kids ready to genuinely get how computers and AI work.

We build real CS foundations first — instructions, logic, loops, data — then reveal what makes AI different, with a quick live AI demo every week to keep the magic alive.

  • → How computers follow precise instructions
  • → If/then logic & decision trees
  • → Loops, data & finding patterns
  • → How AI learns — and when it gets things wrong

Capstone: each Explorer trains their own image-recognition model in Teachable Machine and demos it live on Demo Day.

Explorers B · Grades 6–7 · Ages 11–13

Building with AI

For kids ready for real technical depth and a bigger build.

From the Turing Test to how LLMs predict the next word — including the actual algebra behind how models learn, taught visually so it clicks.

  • → Machine learning & prompt engineering
  • → The math behind learning (functions & error)
  • → LLMs, RAG, AI agents & multimodal AI
  • → Bias, ethics & real-world AI policy

Capstone: an AI Impact Report plus a tangible working prototype — a trained model, chatbot, or design — presented to families.

The final project

Every Explorer builds real AI — then shows it off

The last two weeks are a capstone. It's launched in Week 5, built partly at home, and presented live to families on Demo Day in Week 6. What each Explorer builds depends on their cohort.

Explorers A · Grades 4–5

Teachable Machine Classifier

Train your very own AI image classifier on a topic you choose — then prove it works.

What you build

A 2-class image model in Teachable Machine: thumbs-up vs thumbs-down, smiling vs neutral, your pet vs not your pet — whatever you pick.

The requirements

  • At least 30 training examples per class
  • Tested on 10+ examples it has never seen
  • Written up: your accuracy, one thing it got wrong, and why

Your 2-minute live demo

  • What you classified and why you chose it
  • How you trained it
  • Show it working live — and failing, if you can
  • The one thing that surprised you
Explorers B · Grades 6–7

AI Impact Report + Prototype

Find a real problem, design an AI solution, and build something tangible you can demonstrate.

What you build (pick one)

  • A trained ML4Kids or Teachable Machine model
  • A working chatbot trained with ML4Kids
  • An annotated Figma / slides wireframe of the solution

Plus a full Impact Report

Problem · How My AI Works · Fairness Audit · Ethics & Risks · Next Steps.

Your 7-minute presentation + Q&A

  • The problem and why it matters
  • How your AI works — input, process, output
  • A live demo of your prototype
  • The fairness and ethics concerns you found
  • The math idea behind it, and what you'd build next

Demo Day — it ends with applause, not a worksheet

Week 6 is a family showcase. Every Explorer presents the AI they built, explains how they trained it, and shows it running live (failures included — those are the best part).

What families see
  • ✦ Each kid presents a working AI project they trained themselves
  • ✦ Real explanations: what it learned, where it fails, and why
  • ✦ Peer recognition — Most Creative, Most Surprising, Best Explanation
  • ✦ A "I used to think… now I think…" reflection from every Explorer
